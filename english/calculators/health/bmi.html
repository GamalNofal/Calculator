About Body Mass Index (BMI)

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using a person's height and weight. The formula is BMI = kg/m² where kg is a person's weight in kilograms and m² is their height in meters squared. BMI is used as a screening tool to indicate whether a person is underweight, healthy weight, overweight, or obese.

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5–24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25–29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or greater

Note: BMI is a useful general guideline, but it does not directly assess body fat or health. For a full health assessment, consult a healthcare provider.
